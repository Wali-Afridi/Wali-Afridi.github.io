Project Description

In the summer of 2024, I worked as a research assistant in the Jena-Xing Laboratory for Ultrawide Bandgap Semiconductors at Cornell University. My research focused on characterizing the optical properties of Aluminum Nitride (AlN) thin films. I primarily used photoluminescence spectroscopy, designing experiments, collecting data, and using modelling techniques to try to determine the optical qualities of the films that were grown by other members of the team using Molecular Beam Epitaxy. The purpose of samples was to be used in the fabrication of novel sub 300nm Deep UV laser diodes, a barrier that is yet to be overcome by any electrically pumped diode laser.
